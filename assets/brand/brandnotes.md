# Hello Empathy Brand Notes

## Purpose
These notes describe the current brand direction for the Hello Empathy website rebuild. Use them as a visual and tonal guide when making design decisions.

## Primary logo
- Primary logo file: `assets/brand/logo.jpg`
- The logo features a heart-shaped puzzle mark in gold next to the words "HELLO EMPATHY" with "COUNSELING" below.
- The logo should be treated as the primary brand anchor for the website.

## Brand feel
- Calm
- Warm
- Professional
- Trustworthy
- Compassionate
- Human-centered
- Clean and modern

## Visual direction
- Use a quiet, reassuring visual style.
- Favor spacious layouts with strong breathing room.
- Avoid overly busy backgrounds, harsh contrast, or trendy effects that reduce trust.
- The site should feel polished and welcoming rather than corporate or flashy.
- The overall tone should support emotional safety, clarity, and professionalism.

## Color direction
The current logo suggests a green-and-gold palette with soft neutral support colors.

### Core palette direction
- Deep calming green as the main brand color
- Warm gold as the accent color
- Off-white or soft white for clean backgrounds and readable contrast
- Use darker green tones for depth and lighter green tones sparingly for subtle variation

### Approximate palette starting points
These are starting references only and can be refined by sampling from the actual logo asset if needed.

- Primary green: `#2F5E46`
- Deep green: `#244A38`
- Soft green: `#5F7F6B`
- Warm gold: `#D8BE73`
- Deeper gold: `#B89A4D`
- Soft white: `#F7F5F0`
- Text charcoal: `#2B2B2B`

## Logo usage
- Do not stretch, distort, rotate, or recolor the logo without approval.
- Preserve the logo proportions.
- Keep enough clear space around the logo so it feels prominent and clean.
- Avoid placing the logo on visually noisy backgrounds.
- Use the full logo lockup where appropriate.
- If a simplified mark is ever needed, ask before creating or using one.

## Typography direction
- The current logo suggests a clean, confident, modern typography style.
- Favor readable, professional fonts with warmth and clarity.
- Avoid fonts that feel childish, decorative, or overly clinical.
- Site typography should balance professionalism with emotional accessibility.
- Headings should feel confident and clear.
- Body text should feel calm, readable, and approachable.

## UI and layout guidance
- Favor clear section structure and obvious next steps.
- Keep calls to action visible but not aggressive.
- Use whitespace intentionally.
- Maintain a strong content hierarchy so visitors can quickly understand services and next steps.
- Use soft visual rhythm and clean spacing instead of heavy ornamentation.
- Rounded corners, soft shadows, and gentle section separation are acceptable if used sparingly.

## Photography and imagery guidance
- Favor imagery that feels calm, human, grounded, and respectful.
- Avoid cliché or overly staged mental health imagery.
- Avoid visuals that feel too sterile, too dark, or emotionally heavy.
- If illustrations are used, keep them minimal and aligned with the calm, trustworthy brand feel.

## Copy and tone guidance
- Tone should be warm, clear, reassuring, and professional.
- Avoid overly academic, cold, or jargon-heavy language.
- Avoid overly casual or playful language that weakens trust.
- Make visitors feel understood, informed, and gently guided.

## Implementation guidance
- When making design proposals, reference both `CLAUDE.md` and this file.
- When proposing a new section or page, ensure it aligns with the brand feel above.
- If a design choice pushes the brand in a noticeably different direction, ask first before implementing it.
